# Document 4 — PDF report format

**Greenmentor ESG Applicability & Readiness Tool**

This is the complete specification for the 2-page PDF report that is emailed to the user after they submit the lead capture form. A4 portrait, generated by PDFMonkey from a JSON payload from Make.com.

---

## Format constants

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Format | A4 portrait |
| Pages | 2 |
| Generated by | PDFMonkey |
| Filename pattern | `Greenmentor_ESG_Report_{CompanyName}_{Date}.pdf` |
| Brand colors | Greenmentor green primary, amber/yellow/red/grey for band signalling |
| Primary font | Single sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Open Sans) |
| Page margins | 18mm top/bottom, 20mm left/right (denser than standard to fit content) |

### Page 1 sections explained

#### Header block

| Variable | Source |
|---|---|
| `{Company Name}` | Lead capture / intro screen |
| `{Q1}` | Section 1 of questionnaire |
| `{Q2}` | Section 1 of questionnaire |
| `{Q3}` | Section 1 of questionnaire |
| `{Date}` | System date of PDF generation |

#### Framework Applicability block

Shown in **fixed order**: BRSR (full), BRSR Core, CCTS, CBAM, GRI, Organizational GHG Footprint, Custom / Buyer ESG Reporting.

Each framework displays:
- Icon (● filled green / ● filled amber / ◐ half-filled grey / ○ empty grey)
- Framework name
- Label (Definite / Likely / Possible / Doesn't apply currently)
- Confidence percentage in parentheses
- Why text on the next line (1–2 sentences)

**Why text is auto-selected** from a template library keyed by which rule matched in the applicability evaluator (see Document 6). Approximately 30 templates total — 4–5 per framework.

**Q18 boost acknowledgement:** When the Q18 boost was applied to this framework, append this sentence to the why text:

> *"The active ESG data requests your team has reported suggest this is becoming a practical expectation, not just a theoretical one."*

**CBAM softening note:** When CBAM = Definite, append to the why text:

> *"CBAM applies specifically to steel, aluminium, cement, fertilizers, electricity, and hydrogen-related products. If your EU exports are in a different product category within your sector, this framework may not apply — happy to clarify on a call."*

#### Readiness block

Displays:
- Band name (color-coded; red/amber/yellow-green/green)
- Total score (X / 44)
- Sub-area progress bars with score (X / 12 for A/B/C, X / 8 for D)

| Sub-area | Questions |
|---|---|
| Data infrastructure | Q8 + Q9 + Q10 |
| People & knowledge | Q11 + Q12 + Q13 |
| Governance | Q14 + Q15 + Q16 |
| Output & pressure | Q17 + Q18 |

#### What this means paragraph

Auto-composed paragraph, ≤120 words. Selected from a template library of **28 paragraphs** = 4 bands × 7 weakest-area variants.

Format:
- Opens with a statement about the user's overall position
- Acknowledges the strongest sub-area
- Names the weakest sub-area as the most important focus
- Closes with a forward-looking statement about typical timeframes for movement to the next band

Each of the 28 templates is authored once and reused across all respondents matching that band + weakest area combination.

### Page 2 sections explained

#### Best Practices for Your Sector

Up to 5 bullets. Each bullet 15–25 words. Each ends with `[Source name, publication date]` citation.

**Lookup logic (5-attempt graceful degradation):**

| Attempt | Key | Specificity |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sector × Band × Turnover × Sub-area | Exact match |
| 2 | Sector × Band × Turnover | Drop sub-area |
| 3 | Sector × Band | Drop turnover |
| 4 | Sector | Drop band |
| 5 | "Other" cluster fallback | Sector-agnostic |

First successful lookup wins.

**Bullet selection from matched cell (cell may have up to 7 authored bullets, engine picks up to 5):**

1. Bullets explicitly addressing the user's weakest sub-area — first
2. Bullets touching Definite or Likely frameworks — next
3. Sector-general bullets at this band — fill remaining

If matched cell has fewer than 5 bullets, engine pulls additional from parent (less granular) cell to reach 3–5 bullets total.

#### How ESG-Ready Peers in Your Sector Benefit

3 paragraphs, one per benefit category: **Investor/Banking**, **Customer/Market Access**, **Compliance/Risk**.

Each paragraph 30–50 words. Each ends with `[Source name, publication date]` citation.

**Lookup logic per category (3-attempt graceful degradation):**

| Attempt | Key | Specificity |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sector × Turnover × Category | Exact match |
| 2 | Sector × Category | Drop turnover |
| 3 | "Other" × Category | Sector-agnostic |

If a category has no content at any fallback level, that category section is gracefully omitted (PDF shows 2 paragraphs instead of 3 rather than blank or placeholder).

#### How Greenmentor Can Help (STATIC SECTION)

**This section is identical on every PDF, every respondent.** No engine logic. No personalization beyond the dual CTA.

Static content (full text):

## Edge case behaviours

### Edge case A — All 7 frameworks = "Doesn't apply currently"

**Page 1 framework section:** All 7 shown with ○ icon and "Doesn't apply" label. Why texts explain *why* each framework is currently outside the company's scope.

**Page 1 readiness section:** Runs as normal — score + band + sub-areas + "what this means."

**Page 1 "What this means" paragraph is REPLACED** by this honesty paragraph:

> *"Based on your current profile — unlisted, no exports, no top-250 listed buyers, no global MNC customers — no ESG reporting framework currently applies to your business. ESG infrastructure becomes relevant if you anticipate (a) supplying to listed Indian companies or global MNCs, (b) entering EU exports, (c) raising debt or equity from international lenders, or (d) crossing the ₹500 Cr turnover threshold. If none of these are on your horizon in the next 24 months, ESG reporting is not a near-term priority for you. We'd still suggest a basic GHG inventory as a sensible baseline that future-proofs you, but it isn't urgent."*

**Page 2 sections:**
- Best Practices → "Foundational practices worth considering" (short, generic, GHG-focused)
- Peer Benefits → "When ESG becomes relevant for your sector" — speaks to triggers, not benefits
- "How Greenmentor can help" → unchanged (still static)

### Edge case B — Advanced band (34–44/44) with full applicability

**Page 1:** Standard layout. Why texts and what-this-means paragraph emphasise maturity: third-party assurance, Scope 3 deep-dives, integration of ESG into financial reporting.

**Page 2 Best Practices:** Bullets filtered to focus on optimisation, assurance readiness, value-chain leadership rather than foundational gaps.

**Page 2 Peer Benefits:** Standard.

**"How Greenmentor can help":** Unchanged — static section is identical for all PDFs.

### Edge case C — Mixed readiness (e.g., output high, governance low)

**Page 1 "What this means" paragraph** explicitly flags the mismatch:

> *"Your demonstrated output is ahead of your underlying governance — formalising policy and Board engagement is the natural consolidation step."*

Selected from the 28-template library by matching band + weakest sub-area.

### Edge case D — Designation field blank

PDF doesn't reference designation anywhere. No content variations triggered.

## Visual density rules

To fit up to 5 Best Practices bullets in the available space on Page 2:

- Sub-section headers reduced to single line where possible
- Inter-section dividers thinner (single rule, not block separators)
- "How Greenmentor can help" condensed to 4 single-line offering descriptions (not 4 paragraphs)
- Footer compressed to one line

The visual should still feel professional — denser, not crammed. Every bullet readable. Whitespace minimum-but-sufficient.
